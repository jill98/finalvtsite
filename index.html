The VT-Shaped Individual

A revitalization of the T-Shaped Student throught the intersection of Liberal Arts and Technology
More information available at Beyond Boundaries.

Are you a novel-reading, math-fearing, head-in-the-clouds Liberal Arts student who now finds themself at Virginia Tech, emphasis on the Tech? Do the terms "HTML" and "GitHub" strike fear into your heart, if you know the terms at all?

If it's a yes for the first question, then a warm welcome! Don't be fooled by the name of our school; the Liberal Arts College is a prominent presence on campus, having strong academic curricula for all its majors and talented professors to teach them well. Liberal Arts is the hidden gem of Virginia Tech, and is continuing to grow and thrive!

If it's a yes for the second question, or if eveything about website design is a foreign concept, don't be alarmed! I hope to show how useful these fundamental tools of coding can be, and how rewarding learning a different area of knowledge truly is!

Jill Marney

I, Jill Marney, have developed this website specifically to tell any up-and-coming Liberal Arts students at Virginia Tech how hard it is to develop websites. Just know that I suffered out of love.

As a freshman myself, I feel as though it is important to share the new model for Virginia Tech's Liberal Arts program with any rising first-years entering this field of study. Especially since the VT model emphasizes the use of technology in Liberal Arts, which can be an unappealing area at times for those who prefer more abstract and interpretative studies. I have only been coding for a couple weeks, and, as someone who leans more towards Liberal Arts, I can that intersecting with technology is a grueling challenge, but always a rewarding one.

Before taking the course Writing and Digital Media, I only taught myself the bare minimum of computer applications to skate by. I managed well in Microsoft Programs and Google Docs, but any deeper terrified me. Besides, I was more of a creative learner, drawn to reading, writing, and drawing; there didn't seem to be any point trying to learn the confusing and detailed art of coding, right? Wrong. I did not realize how much I could thrive creatively with even just a basic knowledge of HTML and CSS. And although most days I want to slam my head against my Lenovo if I can't figure something out, nothing in the world beats clicking on your website link and having all your meticulous, grueling hardwork, and not having a 404 Page Not Found pop up instead.

Coding is inherently intuitive and easy to commit to memory. To those who aren't initiually passionate about technological expertise, or to those who aren't detail-oriented, it will be a frustrating and draining process at the start. But success in an area that doesn't come naturally to you will taste so much sweeter than success in areas that you are already proficient. I struggle with website design still, but now I'm not just "managing". I'm thriving.

The Origin of the VT Model

The VT-Shaped Individual is a concept that has been in development at Virginia Tech in order to prepare their students to solve global problems through collaboration and contextual understanding.

The Vertical Component of the T-Shaped Student

The vertical component represents the focus on departmental and disciplinary strengths within specified subjects to deepen understanding. Virginia Tech promotes these ideals through intensive curricula in all areas of study. The enhanced T then allows the spread of technological education to all departments, from engineering to liberal arts.

The Horizontal Component of the T-Shaped Student

The horizontal component promotes interdisciplinary inquiry and complex problem solving and research through collaboration. This component focuses on the interpersonal connections to fellow team members, believing that the most effective and enriching path toward solutions for challenges is through a cross-cutting ecosystem.

The V of the VT-Shaped Student

The V enriches the model through large-scale multi-function partnerships between the college community and respective potential industries. This component brings about both informal communal learning and guided experiential learning through opportunities like internships, research, philanthropies, and education abroad.

My Plans and Goals

While I'm very involved at Virginia Tech, I'm still lacking the full VT-Shaped look. The problem is I'm a textbook case of a right-brained thinker. And I'm finally coming to terms with how stunted I am in other areas, which I wish to develop. I'm going to continue to learn more about coding and anything else that comes my way through this course. I've always wanted to publish the comics I've drawn, so my goal now is to make a functioning website that I can personalize and have complete creative domain over. Also, to achieve the V, I am planning on getting an internship next summer, and study abroad my final semester and Virginia Tech.

Recommendations

I believe it is important to be as involved as possible when you belong to a big campus. However, I also think it's important to be devoted to a handful of activities rather than be half-involved with a hundreds of clubs. Also, diversify what you do! This is why I decided to double major in Finance and Professsional Writing; it's important for your growth to be well-rounded rather than being stuck with one thing. Also, be willing to try new things and go on adventures! Study abroad, interships, a random class that isn't for your major; open yourself up to new things.
